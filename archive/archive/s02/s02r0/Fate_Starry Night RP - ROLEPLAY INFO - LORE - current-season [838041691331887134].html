The waters of the new world had grown indescribably hot in recent times. A sign that summer was coming. Of course, this meant that most of the posh elite was sitting comfortably inside of their cool homes, powered by wind leylines and mystical fans. For them, the volatile impact of the weather might as well be a blot in the ocean. But with the change of seasons came new opportunities. Even in the apocalypse, the value of business never truly died. This particularly warm day further reinforced that, as businessmen, aristocrats, mages and entrepreneurs visited a Dhruva located in some small corner in the Earth. It was a resort for the rich. A place for them to engage in side activities befitting men of such caliber. Today, it was auctions. A dozen or so men sat inside a building clearly designed to resemble a chapel. The sponsor for this bidding session was none other than the Neo Holy Empire, a remnant of the Holy Church that has been making strides in the last few decades. As such, their name was now commonplace among the elite, with some aristocrats even being part of the Holy Order. The offer for today? An entire city. Or the remnants of one, at the very least. It was once called Persepolis, but now it's nothing more than a wandering wreck waiting to be pillaged. The Church has cordoned off the area around it to prevent scavengers from intruding and is now offering it to the highest bidder. (edited)
"Three thousand." "Five thousand." "One million." Numbers were tossed around faster than a calculator. Who knew what currencies they were using. Whether they matched the actual value of the city or not. What mattered was that all men fell silent after a few minutes, save for two men who were still raising their voices to each other. "Two billion." "Three billion." "Three point five billion." "Three point six-" "You don't have that much. Stop bluffing." A man in a pure white getup said as they slicked back their blond hair. It was none other than Olympia's president, a famous aristocrat by the name of Mr. Irons. As his namesake implied, he owned quite the sizable ironworks factory. A whole bunch of them, to be precise. "I don't think this bid said anything against loans now, did it?" A slightly shorter man replied with a visible babyface, made even more uncanny by their elegant and refined suit that would fit most businessmen. Just what you would expect from Gill Bates, a famous billionaire and entrepreneur that controls numerous shares in Delphi's businesses. The two of them were clearly vying for the prize. Knowing that they would go on for ages, a wise member of the Church intervened. "Ahem, gentlemen. It is clear that these bids have gone far beyond monetary terms. At this rate, both of you might go bankrupt, which defeats the purpose of this auction. Instead, the Church proposes another alternative." The nun said with such grace that she immediately put the two bidders at ease, if only for a moment. "You two are major sponsors of two different cities, correct? Then the solution is simple." - Season 2 - Autumn 2104. Several months since Persepolis had fallen. Olympia's fumes were obnoxious. While the factories were prolific and led to the city's overall growth in terms of wealth and size, the amount of pollution caused in return was disastrous. There was no way for it to sustain itself or grow bigger, unless enough scrap could be gathered to create a new portion of the city specifically dedicated to filter waste. Likewise, Delphi had enough of being called 'the little guy'. A city thriving on businesses and stocks was considered laughable in this post-apocalyptic age. Numbers and money only held value if there was no need for blood to be spilled. The only way people would take Delphi seriously was if it increased its size and presence around the globe. Of course these two cities would fight over the same prize. It was on the news. It was on papers. It was on the City Wide Net. Visitors from all around the globe were flocking to these two cities that would normally be miles apart. This time, however, they shared a single location. After all, they had temporarily docked with one another. It was in preparation for the most ambitious event of the current year. Olympia v Delphi: City Grail War Who will win? Who will fall? Place your bets now!
Clearly, someone was taking advantage of the rampant advertising going on. Apparently, the two cities were fighting over the wreckage of a Dhruva. They decided to settle it through a Holy Grail War, with participants divided into two factions: Olympia and Delphi. However, this would not be a normal Holy Grail War: It would be a tournament. A televised event. One where both Olympia and Delphi's might would shine. It was both in the aristocracy and stakeholders' interests, after all. The rules were still being ironed out. The arenas were still being developed. The contestants were still being gathered. But a lot of gossip had already formed around the grand prize... A wish. Not from a Holy Grail, but a material wish that could be obtained through resources and money. Any magus would fall for that bait. No matter their rank. In just a few weeks, these mages would gather. All of them from different cities and backgrounds as they fought to grasp their own destiny...
