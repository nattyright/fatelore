What was it like, to act as overseer for a factory designed to end human lives? Not a bad little job. Not great, either. The paychecks were good enough, but there was no progression, no prestige. Once you got a grip on the ladder of Olympia, there was no climbing up. You got to sit in a cramped office, doing piles of mundane paperwork, watching smoke and embers billow outside your window, hearing the lamentations of the workers. All the while, MacKenzie was enjoying all the fruits of your labours, all while exerting a decimal of the effort you put in. That was all about to change. Laurent MacKenzie. Your ivory tower is about to topple, and I’ll be the one that knocks it down. I’m going to take everything away from you. I’m going to gain everything that you lose, and there’s nothing you can do about it.
“How the hell did you get it?” Gerard Russo sat in his office chair as he spoke into his phone, idly swivelling back and forth. The rest of his furniture had been pushed behind him, pressed against the walls and stacked to the ceiling to make room for his summoning circle. In the centre of it all, was his S.E.R.V.A.N.T. He looked up at it, then fished a card out of his pocket. A license of sorts, a denotation of the serial number of this particular frame. SBRM004401 “Why, whatever could you mean?” Came the reply, a feminine voice crackling through the phone. There was playfulness in her voice, a tone that was simultaneously a light-hearted rib and a deep mockery. “Do you doubt the validity of my sources, dear little Russo?” “Of course I do! You and I both know this guy got corrupted after the floods. They couldn’t salvage his Spirit Origin. So, how the fuck did you get him?” A giggle, a wriggling laughter that only irritated the foreman. She was being coy. “Well, I can’t give away all my secrets, can I? Just trust that I’ll do right by you, after you did right by us. Remember, we get 50% of the profits of your victory. Of course I’m going to give you the best you can get, because that’s the best that I can get. Sheesh, aren’t you Olympia types supposed to be smart about this business stuff?” The woman replied, the smirk on her face evident in her voice. She wasn’t wrong. It was hard to get S.E.R.V.A.N.T rigs at this time, especially if you weren’t a billionaire. So, he had to go under the radar, into the black market. There was no doubt he was dealing with criminals, and no doubt that his Saber would be stolen goods. That wouldn’t matter, though. What were they going to do, persecute him? Once he had won this Grail War, he could do whatever he wanted. That was what mattered.
