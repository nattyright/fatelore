Laurent MacKenzie BOT 13-Aug-21 08:10 AM
The air was clean, if only for a brief moment. The rain that had resulted from Olympia's damaged weather dome had resulted in Olympia's air quality going from a whimpering one to a resounding ten. It was incredible what a little water could do. It was unfortunate, then, that Laurent had little time to experience it. Following his escape from the Brobdingnagian, Laurent had been rushed to Olympia Medical Hospital's ER, and had been operated on instantly. If someone as important as a MacKenzie had died on the hospital's watch, their reputation wound be damaged, perhaps forever. Medical professionals and Servants alike were brought in. None were on the same level as a famed clinic in Delphi, but combined, they were enough to stabilize a man suffering from overloaded magic circuits. If his singularity had tried to absorb any more of Kun Wu's Magecraft, Laurent would've lost his legs, and more than likely, his life. He had been lucky. MacKenzie Industries, on the other hand, quietly chugged along. Laurent did not show up to any shareholder meetings, nor was he available for those to discuss contracts with both Olympia and Delphi. However, these were the same people who had steered the company through the disastrous ring-around that Laurent's father had brought on, and they were more than capable of taking over for a few days. After a few days, Laurent was quietly allowed to leave OLH, and was to be watched for a short while by trained staff, though they were quickly replaced by Laurent's own men. It hurt to walk.
That was the first thought that Laurent had as he staggered out of a meeting with his shareholders. Well, "stagger" was a strong word. He limped with style, as one befitting his position. He was strong, yet just weak enough for others to sympathize with his struggle. When he reached the elevator leading to his private quarters, Laurent fell to the ground, breathing heavily. Would I ever fully recover? Was the only thought that rushed through his head. That, and lamentation that he was so frail. That brat and his concubine. They had done this to him. How he wanted to get revenge, but he couldn't. He was too weak. Clumsily getting to his feet, he staggered down the hall to his personal quarters. When he got there, he grabbed a flower pot stand by the door for dear life. He eyed the next nearest source of support: a leather recliner. He lunged for it. Thud. He hit the ground in a grunt of pain. This was going to be difficult. He wasn't supposed to use Magecraft for a few days, lest his circuits fry again. Laurent groaned, and resigned himself to crawling across the floor like a worm. Upon reaching his study, he clawed his way up onto his seat, and put his head in his hands. He had to think. Think of some way to come out of this City Grail War with something. Apparently, a Delphian businessman who worked with hotels had been attacked, and his hotel had been damaged. Perhaps he could get a contract with the Kingsmades, along with offering to buffer up their security team's equipment. However, he had bigger issues. Issues closer to home. Something like the massive castle that he could see from his window. It was getting larger. It was an eyesore, and the fact that it was so close meant that it would one day become a battleground that could affect MacKenzie Industries's infrastructure. It had to be dealt with.
However, nobody from Delphi would care to march across two cities just to fight a castle that Saber and his Master would leave. It had to be an Olympian. Which meant that it was finally time to meet someone that had been on Laurent's mind for a week. Lark and his Lancer. Meeting them now would take too long. He was weak and tired. He rung up his secretary. Yes? A serious female voice answered. "Hey, Serenity. Clear my schedule for tomorrow. Let the senior council know, and let them take over in my stead. No decisions are final until I confirm them." Yes, Mr. MacKenzie. The phone hung up, and Laurent sighed. Hopefully his legs functioned tomorrow.
