Gustave could hardly stifle a yawn as the sunlight lazily filtered through his bedroom's shades. It had been several days since that affair with the maddened violinist and the conqueror who almost quenched his life before his very eyes. That was the second time he had came close to death's door, the second time he had made a mistake. As he laid under his sheets in an awkward repose, his mind couldn't help but to drift back to that first incident. Yes the circumstances were very different but that lack of caution, of measured distrust in a dangerous environment had punished him dearly. The first time he had run into Toulouse too, he doubt he could have seen again without his intervention. Why had he allowed himself to slip up? He was not at a moment of great triumph, he had simply exposed himself at the wrong time and gambled his life on the off chance to spite the enemy master. He could not allow such rash thoughts enter his mind again, and as much as he was loath to admit it, he would have to tap back into that cold, uncaring and inhuman mindset that the Mage Academy had impressed into him so long ago. Mercy, kindness, such ideas would be subordinate to the principles of risk and reward, and the chance at victory above all must be maintained. He would have to worry about any moral conundrums after it was all done, that was what the painting was for. A slight tremor in his leg brought his attention back to the material plane and the second miracle Toulouse had peformed on him in his lifetime. He could walk so soon after such a grievous injury but his legs still ached. Perhaps today, he would benefit from a long walk, further compose his thoughts and work out a plan of action. Another letter, right after his injury, Gustave already didn't like the parallels this was drawing. Surely it had to be the Salon letting him know of the incident and reminding him of his debt. Yet what he found was unexpected. As he read, he could feel the ache in his legs intensify, the occasional twitch sending tremors up his body, leaving goosebumps behind. Somehow, someone had discoverd his secret arrangement with Lemuria's mystical and secretive Salon and now sought to use it as some sort of leverage. It was the cherry on top of their ploy, spending a great deal bragging about their wide net of informations. However he was dealing with had wealth and power approaching that of his contractors and with far greater ambitions. The writer's demands were vague, promising some type of partnership to levee their connections in the painter's favor. There was no way stipulations and conditions were awaiting him, those in power always were eager for more pawns and yet another wanted him as their own. The thought of being manipulated by two groups and played against one was enough to bring Gustave's mind off the healing injuries and onto the promise of uncovering this unknown factor and plumbing its depths. Yes a partnership could be useful for the time being, but Gustave already knew that this arrangement would only last so long before the knives would begin to dig into his backer. And so he grabbed cane and cap, and set out on his way once more towards the city of rulers...
