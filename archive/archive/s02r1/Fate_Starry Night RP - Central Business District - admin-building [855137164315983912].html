Late Summer, a few weeks after the bidding war between Delphi and Olympia.
A long table extended in the middle of the pristine meeting room. At the end of it stood the man that oversaw the Governing body of Delphi, Frederic Figglehorn. At either sides of him sat the member of said council. They were the ones dictating the rules Delphi citizens and businesses had to follow and they closely monitored the economic activities that transpired inside and outside Delphi. All in all, they were the big boys that governed the island. Although the room was perfectly clean, there were flies in every corners of the room, subtle enough not to raise anyone's attention. "I hope you have a good reason to assemble us on such short notice, Frederic." One of the older members spoke, clearly irritated. "Delphi's activities have drastically changed ever since the news of the battle for Persepolis has been made public, we should be out here monitoring all that. This is not the right time to-" Frederic raised his hand, stopping him in his tracks. "It is about the upcoming Holy Grail War and our involvement in it." The council members suddenly perked up at that answer, giving their full attention to Frederic. "You see," He spoke, leaning forward. "We have finally decided on a contender to aid us in the battle for Persepolis. We checked his background and have found no red-flags and he seems to perfectly fit in with our standards as far as Magus go. You can read about his profile on the pamphlets that we will provide you." He snapped his fingers and an assistant entered the room, giving out pamphlets to each council member. "This is not the kid you talked us about in our last meeting." A woman said as she skimmed through the paper. "This one seems dull in comparison."
"Kun Wu," Frederic answered. "The negotiations didn't go well. He thought the job was 'too boring' for him, decided to decline our offer. This one, though, should accept without concerns. We have already sent him a message along with half the revenue we promised him for the job. We should receive his answer shortly, today if I'm not mistaken. Of course, we've also prepared the S.E.R.V.A.N.T chassis along with the catalyst and the stock of Mana batteries needed for him to summon and sustain his Servant."
The bugs stirred a bit, as if reacting to Frederic's statement. The council members started talking amongst themselves as soon as he finished, arguing about the man described in the pamphlets. All in all, they thought he looked strong and reliable, an experienced Magus that could very well end the war in a victory for them. "I'm glad our investment didn't go to waste. We spent a lot on that chassis at a crucial juncture in the development of our city. That catalyst cost a pretty penny, too." One of the men said. The others nodded in acknowledgement. "You can rest easy, people." Frederic assured. "That investment will result in a huge benefit for us, I can guarantee that." (edited)
At the same time, at a coffee shop near the Admin Building.
"Tehee..." Sitting on a table outside a coffee shop, a blonde girl laughed. Her name was Laura Hess. She had a book between her hands, stopping ever so often to take a sip of her black Arabica. Her focus wasn't on her book, though. She concentrated on what the flies inside the meeting room saw and heard. Hundreds, thousands of tiny windows that gave away different angles and dispositions of the politicians inside. Her secondary power served to analyse and filter anything she didn't need, leaving only the good bits of information to enter her brain. She saw their every move and recorded every sentence uttered, all through her bugs. She was a Magus. "Ya can't say stuff like that and not expect to get screwed somewhere along the way, Frederic. You'd think that a cunning man that rose through the ranks in a competitive city like Delphi would understand that nothing is ever guaranteed. Guess all that power went over your head..." Laura said to no one in particular. She had a habit of monologuing ever so often. It started out as a dumb joke she made to herself and ended up an ingrained habit. She didn't mind how weird it looked, though. She thought it was fun, talking like some dumb comic-book villain. Closing her book and finishing her cup of coffee, she stood up from her chair and went her merry way. There was still a lot to do for Laura and time was short until the Holy Grail War started.
Fall, three days before the start of the first matches.
"There's three days left, Frederic!" One of the council members exclaimed. "This man you talked us about accepted our offer, didn't he? Where is he, then? Was he scared? Did he run for his life, perhaps?!" The man bombarded him with questions, asking each with more and more intensity. Frederic stood from his chair, lightly massaging the bridge of his nose. Some of the council men around him looked uneasy, others were irritated. Only he stood undisturbed and composed at the center of them. He calmly leaned forward on the table, fingers intertwined. "I understand your anger," He spoke. "But doubting a professional will do us no good. The man has a good reputation. He'll do any job for the right amount of money and we have given him the right amount of money. There's no reason for him to run away. He is probably preparing himself for the war as we speak." "Without taking the S.E.R.V.A.N.T chassis nor the catalyst used for the summoning?" Someone retorted. Silence lingered for a few seconds as the council members looked at each other in disbelief. Before someone could speak again, the doors of the meeting room suddenly opened. A secretary entered the room, a letter in hand. "We've received a message from the Magus, sir!" She spoke, crossing the room and giving the letter to Frederic. He took a look at it, then at his fellow council men. "What a great timing." He said with a curt and assured smile. The men relaxed a tiny bit at that, laughing amongst themselves as if everything was smooth sailing from now. (edited)
Frederick tore the letter and took the single sheet of paper inside it, skimming rapidly through it. "What does it say?" Someone asked. Frederic raised an eyebrow. He read the letter a second time, a third time. His smile faded away. "He quit." He answered. Silence reigned as the men processed those two words. "What?!" One of them exclaimed, slamming his fist on the table. The others followed with shouts and angry cries, some of them directed at the Magus, others at Frederic. "Gentlemen," He raised his voice so he could be heard over the shouts. "He said he couldn't make it in time for the Holy Grail War. That does not mean that he leaves us completely naked. This letter says that he had prepared a contingency plan in case he couldn't do the job himself. He specifically says that he entrusted the job to another skilled Magus in which he has absolute faith in. There's no setback to our plan." Before the council men could even voice an opinion, Frederick folded the paper stood from his seat. "I will now personally see this person and clarify things with them. After all, I am taking responsibility for any unexpected developments. Now, if you'll excuse me..." He swiftly leaved the room, quietly followed by the secretary. "Prepare a car right now." Frederic asked the secretary. She nodded once then leaved his side. He tightly clenched the paper in his hand.
