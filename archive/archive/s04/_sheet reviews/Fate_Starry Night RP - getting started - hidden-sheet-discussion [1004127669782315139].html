Journey through the Alps I like the new changes and the justification accompanying it seems reasonable enough for me. However, I would change it from a two use Noble Phantasm to single use. My reasoning is this ability is far too convenient with two uses per thread, since it allows you to use the first use for a surprise attack, while the second use is essentially a free 'get out of jail' card. The range limit prevents Hannibal from leaving the city, but fighting in one area and then disappearing to the complete opposite side of the map is enough to escape most foes. Because of this, I believe this Noble Phantasm works best as once per thread. That way, you will have to decide on whether to preserve it as a way to guarantee you and your Master's escape, or as a way to catch an opponent off guard and deliver a lethal blow. In my view, it would lead to much more interesting RP dynamics for you and your opponent, along with being thematically fitting with Hannibal: After all, she was known for advancing, not for retreating. Campaign Longevity I feel like I still need more details here. You do state that injuries heal faster for Hannibal, along with the fact that she able to resist the pain, but I also cannot tell how fast they heal and how much pain she can resist. I know that it is hard to quantify these things, but considering Servants in Fate heal naturally as long as their Masters have mana, I kind of need to be able to tell the difference between natural healing and healing attributed to this skill. Speaking of, does the Master or mana output affect this skill's performance in any way? Let's say if Hannibal completely ran out of mana after using both of her NPs. What then?
