Left step, right step, left step, right step… A teenager wearing sunglasses and a face mask zigzagged through the huge crowd that populated the Culinary Bazaar, making full use of his small size to squeeze his way to safety. The people he was jostling were complaining at every turn, but he paid them no attention and picked up the pace again. It had been hours since Thomas had started running. It seemed that the security staff at the ServantPedia™ Head Offices really made it a life goal to catch him, even if it meant vacating their spots in the actual building. An endless cycle of running, getting to safety, and then being jumped by the men brought Thomas to the edge of his endurance. He nearly gave up several times, but the idea that they might not only catch him, but also find out that he was the man who failed Alexandria? It was that fear of being found out that kept him going, even when his legs were screaming and his lungs were threatening to collapse.
03:55
Now, he found himself between two food carts in a secluded part of the bazaar, finally catching his breath after apparently losing his pursuers. He wiped the sweat from his forehead and removed his face mask, which was drooly and steamy from all the running he had done while wearing it. It wasn't until he finally rested that he realized how far he had pushed his body. Once the pressure died down, his legs suddenly gave out and he toppled to the ground, lying in a starfish position, too exhausted to stand anymore. Ah, how lucky he was to have Lewis around back then. This situation reminded him too much of the angry mobs that chased them out of the Asclepeion hospital. Back then, Lewis easily slid through the streets with his nimble legs. It barely took him a couple of minutes to lose the crowds. He made it look so easy. But when Thomas tried to do the same thing, even hours of running and hiding couldn't outrun his pursuers. They stuck to him like hot glue, and there was little he could do about it. If they found him now, he wouldn't be able to get up and run again. This was the end of the road. He closed his eyes as he tried to calm his breathing, as if silently surrendering to his fate.
