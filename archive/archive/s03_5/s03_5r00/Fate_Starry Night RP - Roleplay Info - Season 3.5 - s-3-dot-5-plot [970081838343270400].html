2069 - Republic Testing Labs (Persepolis Branch)
08:03
It was a clear day. No overcast. Average temperatures. Lack of turbulent winds. Perfect, sterile conditions that were as pure as they could be. Persepolis' current weather dome was a thing of wonder, capable of changing the climate in select areas of the city instead of the entire structure. Thanks to this, the entire rest of the week was clear skies ahead. S.E.R.V.A.N.T. frames had been carefully shipped over from one of Republic's many warehouse cities. Packed in large, metal crates with fragile signs plastered all over them, along with runes that deterred any prospective robbers, unless they planned on carrying a curse with them. "Careful there." A researcher ordered, waving their arms around like a conductor as they guided the workers into dropping the crates in the designated drop zone. The testing area was wide and spacious, but it was foolish to summon several Servants all in the same location. Not only did it make it easier for the Servants to find each other, but in case an all-out brawl started, then the fight would be far too chaotic and uneven to gather good training data. Not to mention, these frames were still imperfect. Summoning them out in the open, even with a summoning circle and a Master to support them, had a 73% failure rate. By using the laboratories' additional tools, the failure rate decreased to 42%. Still not great, but that was the price one paid to test the new models designed by Republic. The previous models were not only limited by the amount of Grail data available at disposal, but also artificial limiters designed to keep a Servant's mana signature down to reasonable levels. Of course, that wasn't enough for Republic's goals. They demanded higher performing Servants, hence these tests. The frames required on field testing to iron out any imperfections and difficulties. Better to do so in an open field than to have a frame blow up in a researcher's face.

Hours later...
08:03
All frames had been unpacked from their crates and placed in the laboratories. The preparations had commenced: Summoning circles were formed, mana batteries were prepared and an artificial Master/Servant connection designed for the sole purpose of summoning a Servant was now on standby. With the press of a button, the connection would send a jolt to all of the Servants connected to the laboratory mainframe. This would lead to a chain summon of multiple Servants at once, before severing the connection and letting them act masterless. Of course, a failsafe was also embedded in this connection. With the press of another button, a kill switch could be enabled to disable all the summoned Servants. Specific Servants could be disabled via a command terminal, so long as one remembered the Servant's specific ID. As a result, sticky notes with IDs written on them were rather common in the workplace, as unethical as they were. "How are the testing arenas looking?" A researcher asked, sipping from their cup of evening coffee as their droopy eyes could barely remain open. The job of a Republic scientist was satisfying in many ways, except for working hours. "All of them are ready. Persepolis gave us ample room to work with, due to the recent string of demolitions they have planned. Something about building a shopping mall." Another one pointed out, showing a 3D map diagram of the various battle arenas they had planned. "Sheesh, I wonder if they know that we are leaving next year. You think we should give them a heads up?" "Already have." The researcher replied with a sigh. "But those aristocracy types are living on cloud nine. Nothing I say can wake them up from that dream. Why bother am I right?" He sipped some more before looking at the clock in the corner of the computer monitor. "Tests should be fine to start tomorrow then, right?" "Yep. Should be alright. Just be ready for some grumpy Servants, I guess." ...

A Day Later
08:03
One minute, a flash of magic. Ten seconds later, vague consciousness. By the time the Servants had woken up, they were all in different areas, restraints attached to their limbs that kept them from moving freely. While they looked like cuffs, they felt more like gigantic burdens, preventing their Spirit Origins from moving freely. "Good morning, Heroic Spirits of humanity. I commend you all for showing up here, out of your own volition. After all, we did not use any specific catalysts to summon you." A voice echoed from various sources. A large TV screen. An intercom. A radio. Each area had at least one mouthpiece out at the ready. "To manifest yourself here in your S.E.R.V.A.N.T. frames is the sign that you have accepted Republic's goal: To protect humanity. To avenge the world that rightfully belonged to us. I am honored by your presence, so I shall keep things brief to save you time. You have all been summoned here for a training exercise, akin to a Holy Grail War in terms of structure. Your objective is simple: Kill all other Servants on this island. Dozens of Heroic Spirits lurk just beyond the safety of your summoning circle. In order to be considered the victor of this Grail War, you must defeat all of them. Do whatever it is you see fit to achieve your goals. Surrender will end in death, so make your choices wisely." To the Servants, it all sounded odd, until the felt the Grail data in their frames slowly feed back information to their mind. Knowledge of the world. The flood. The current state of mankind and the nomadic cities. Details on Republic and its objective were also disclosed, along with the purpose of the experiment. It all felt clearer now. They were brought here to fight each other and die. To affirm superiority and test humanity's technological limits. A noble goal, one that a Servant of humanity was obligated to follow through. ...Or at least, pretend to.
