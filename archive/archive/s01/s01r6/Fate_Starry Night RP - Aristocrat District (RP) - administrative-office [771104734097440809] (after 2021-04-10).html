The administration had been in an uproar for hours. Everyone was bustling about, with phones ringing, documents flying from desk to desk, staff rushing about yelling and cursing under their breaths. The War had all but exhausted their resources, then some idiotic enforcer from the Church went and blew up their lifeline. Now they had a whole new faction breathing down their necks, proclaiming to be their salvation only when they were on the verge of collapsing. Could the day get any worse? Tap, tap 'Hello...?' A woman in a frilly uniform invited her into the chaotic space, startling everyone - not by herself, but with the gigantic creature bobbing around behind her. The black jellyfish had managed to squeeze itself through the front door, and was now taking up all the available space in the lobby while leaving ink trails everywhere. The paintings on the wall, ruined. The documents strewn across the floor, unintelligible. The staff, pissed. 'Hey, Miss, this place is off-limits!' One of them approached her, making dismissive gestures and trying to get her to leave. The woman clearly wasn't used to being the center of attention. 'But your front door was-' She started to explain, but the staff cut her off. 'You need to make a reservation first-' 'But I already-' 'Your familiar needs to go-' 'I don't think you'll like-' Thwack The jellyfish whipped one of its tendrils around and smacked the staff in the face, coating them from head to toe in black ink.
That shut the staff up. '-Here!' The woman seized the moment of silence and withdrew a letter from under the frills of her dress. On the back of the letter was an ornate wax seal, complete with a feminine figure holding a pen and set of parchment scrolls. 'I have a letter of introduction. I am here on official business!' She pressed the letter into the staff's hands, then took a deep breath and started reciting the script she had practiced in front of a mirror dozens of times before coming to Persepolis. 'Please take this to your governor. Inform them that a representative from Alexandria has arrived for the acquisition of Persepolis' database. Naturally, the price is negotiable. We can even introduce you to a few of our partners. They would happily take the city off your hands for another handsome sum.' Phew. That was flawless! Well, save for the entrace. Satisfied with her delivery, the woman pulled her book out from another one of the many pockets under her frills. The jellyfish pooled a few of its tendrils together into a makeshift couch. The woman leaned into the seat. 'Take your time, don't worry about me! I promise Kurage will be a good boy.' She reassured the staff, then flipped the book to where she had left off. She was ready to make herself at home...how troublesome. @S1
