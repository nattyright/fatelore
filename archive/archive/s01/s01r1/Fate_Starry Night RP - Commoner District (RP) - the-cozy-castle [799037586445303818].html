Silver and iron to the origin. Let stone and the archduke of contracts be the foundation. The alighted wind becomes a wall. A few seconds passed. He breathed heavily as his frustration slowly drained like the blood from his forehead. Nothing. Absolutely nothing. Wrath turned to regret, which turned to defeat. He slumped back to the floor, hands by his side. He could feel the crimson starting to push past his eyebrows and dribble past his eyes. “...what more do I need to show I can do this?” he asked, staring past the feet of the frame into some unknown distant land. His attention was stolen back as something caught his eye. A flicker of some sort. No, that was just some blood starting to get in his eye. He sighed deeply and reached over for the rag he had dropped earlier. And then chaos ensued. He was thrown back by the circle sparking to life, violently draining him of his magical energy. His back his the far wall as energy swirled inside the circle, a deep red whirlwind engulfing the machine. He could only watch in fear and confusion as magic began to take shape, filling out the details of the frame. Gone were the shiny metal bits and pieces all around, replaced with flesh and fabrics. Form was given to that without, until all that was left was a person much like any other human, but extremely different. A servant.
