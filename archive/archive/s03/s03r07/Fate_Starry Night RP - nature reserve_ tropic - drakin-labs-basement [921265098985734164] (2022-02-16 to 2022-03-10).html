Darkness once again fell like a curtain of soundless satin over Banshee’s eyes and all pain faded into the abyss. There in the void, that indeterminate ocean, she was left alone with her thoughts, savants of torture on a caliber far above any distilled product Caster could produce. In voices numbering 25, they lashed against their owner with whips of regret, knives of scorn and vices of self-denial. She had committed a cardinal sin against her existence, her duty. Abandoning it in a haze of madness and lust to feed that beast she swore to contain. It was so easy to blame it on the berserker she fought but was it not her prerogative that the joining with her master continued even as the haze lifted? Why did she deserve to have love when it could only flicker for a moment before she would have to mourn their death. Love was rebellion against her duty, preferring the company of one over the distant watch of the many. So she walked through the congealed nothingness, seeking nothing but a forced march through its infinite lengths and stay herself in silent contemplation and excoriation of her mind and soul. Death was not hers to have, for how could her task end when life demanded death in an eternal cycle. All those that died deserved one last song to mark their passing and memory. Love would only tarnish and stain the impartiality needed. Eventually she came to that sea once more, feeling the waters try to lap away at her earthen skin and stolid soul. The distorted sky above seemed primed for sunrise, ethereal pastels dancing above the water but the dominance of reddish hues would only serve to put her on edge and deepen her pessimism.
There was no wreath of flame or call of crow to signify her old master nary that giant’s corpse to who she felt a deep, abiding tie. All that lay below her pale feel was an inky blackness that seemed to writhe and contort, bubbling up capsules of foul thoughts and negative memories. Her armored hands violently shuddered as a red bubble shot by and mocked her with a glimpse of a smiling man weaving romance with his lyrical voice. She breathed deeply of the nonexistent waters and exhaled the thoughts it conjured out of her mind just as she had done for centuries before. The mass below replied by sending up a veritable column of those consumed so long ago, their own twisted endings as humanity collapsed in on itself under such existential threat. It made Banshee want to scream more than she had done before. Anger and sorrow mixed within her mind with her own doubts and helplessness. The pressure seemed to come from all sides, nausea and acid corroding her insides. Of course, all that would come out were bubbles of emotion-soaked air to join the rest… The ebb and flow seemed to last into perpetuity, a hellish purgatory meant to trap the mourner of the lost with the memories she so sought to preserve and her own internal losses. When she could scream no more, she found her spiritual body sinking down into the depts to be covered once more. No soil or slime crawled over top but the corpses of the recently departed found their way into those depths with her and in every eye socket, a remnant of soul sought shady refuge or perhaps diminished memories of people trapped in muddy cells… They stretched on for many yards amidst the muddy ocean bed with Banshee, yet their patterns were both haphazard and meticulously organized. The muddy mass they made seemed less and less like what she believed to be incomprehensible.
The floors seemed to harden, the sparse vegetation widening and taking on new geometric patterns, the scant light becoming harsher and more concentrated. One constant, however, was the wailing of those condemned to the bottom with her. A few sung slowly of a new life, many mumbling a monotonous dirge of servitude and a few others still silent and unmoving. She took her time to catalogue each one’s distinct song and memorize each’s soliloquy. It eased her pain and made her slowly forget her own pains and tribulations but there was one voice that did not conform to the others, one that seized upon her private thoughts that oozed out from her despondent form and sought to drag it back onto the path destined. This was a voice she did not expect, one that she had overlooked on that fateful day and one that would bring her back to the living…
