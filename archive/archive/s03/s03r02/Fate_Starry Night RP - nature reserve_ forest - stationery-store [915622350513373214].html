February 3, 2105 - An hour after William Shakespeare made contact with Ace
The stationery was one of Nirvana's safe havens, being the one most connected to the island's past history of tourism, just behind the #merch-store. Despite the amount of time that had passed, the store's assortment of flashy paraphernalia had yet to run dry. Flyers filled with descriptions of Nirvana's habitats and map, books on the number of animals and nature reserves that inhabited the island, along with far more tacky items such as animal plushies (which often ran out of stock). Even more peculiar was the amount of merch that went unsold, usually excess cargo from the merch store that was stored at the stationery for safekeeping due to the short distance between the two. These ranged from keychains, figurines, stickers and even pins, of the Servants that formed Nirvana's entertainment troupe. The indomitable Red Hare, a fan favorite that tended to the more equine animals in the reserves. The fierce huntress, Atalanta, who received disproportionate amounts of merch despite her insistence on avoiding the public eye. The playful demon, Mephistopheles, whose magic shows were quite popular, albeit abruptly cancelled after a short notice. The list went on, until you landed into more niche figures. Since few people on Nirvana saw themselves as talented writers, they decided to outsource their advertising tasks to Servants instead. Literary geniuses such as Hans Christian Andersen, Edgar Allan Poe, Fyodor Dostoevsky, Shi Nai'an... William Shakespeare.
