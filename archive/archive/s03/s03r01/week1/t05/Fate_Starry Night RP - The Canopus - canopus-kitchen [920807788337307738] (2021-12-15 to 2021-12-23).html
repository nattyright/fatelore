Ryn BOT 17-Dec-21 09:30 AM
It's been 5 days. 5 days since the Canopus made its last pick up stop. 5 days since it left land. 5 days since it set sail to Nirvana... and most pressingly... 5 days since Ryn Nuello has eaten a proper meal. See, it hadn't exactly occurred to the young man-- Ryn that is-- that he should pack very much food for his little voyage. Just a slip of his mind really; honestly, clueless as to how long ship-travel took, he imagined the ride would be rather short. So, he'd only brought the amount one would need for a car-trip: about two sandwiches and a bottle of water. A quaint snack. Oh, how he was paying for such a decision now. Naive to the longevity of the ride to Nirvana, Ryn had quickly exhausted himself of his lacking food supply; in quite possibly the worst way possible, too. His first sandwich was devoured before he'd even stepped foot onto the Canopus, and the second, nibbled on happily in celebration of sneaking on. "Hooray, hooray!", he had cheered in whispers... just as he'd finished his last bite of sandwich.  It'd seemed like a good idea at the time, and certainly felt wonderful to take that last mouthful in, all the while peeking out from a hole punctured below deck, and watching the harbor turn into the endless sea. The whole scene had felt a little magical, like the beginning of a story about some vagrant stowaway, making his way to a land of mystery and answers...
- Alas, it only took 5 days for reality to set in. Come day two Ryn was lucky to only get a bit hungry-- by the time the fifth day came? Along with the most unbearable aches of his stomach, he was getting downright delirious. All reason had departed his mind, and in its place had come a vehement, half-witted urge to find food. Though... his body wasn't quite agreeing. Ryn's legs were dizzy as they carried him up from below deck, where he'd stayed hidden just incase the crew members could tell he didn't belong here, and only got wobblier the farther he walked. Luckily, it was the wee hours of the morning-- right about 2 am to be exact-- so no one should've been up... and even if someone was? Ryn didn't care anymore. Catch him, call him out, and throw him overboard for all he cared, he could swim. Just give him a bite to eat first... Eventually, by some miracle, Ryn stumbled his way into the kitchen. He instantly recognized where he was, and invigorated by such, he quickly went to shuffling through the shelves, cupboards, and closets. Pots... pans... knifes... sporks... bowls... plates... but no food. He let out a groan. As much as he would if he could, Ryn couldn't eat any of those things; metal probably wouldn't digest very well. Damnit! The lad continued to shuffle through the kitchen with desperation, not bothering to close the things he opened, and leaving shelves ajar like some wild animal. Not like that was terribly inaccurate. He'd been called a runt all the time back home, what difference did it make here? None! That was what, none at--
- Suddenly, Ryn gasped. Before him, almost radiating with the sterile kitchen light shining on it from above, was a shelf full of canned food. Ryn could feel his heart pace as his hand reached up to grab one, and when he shakily brought it down to read the label, his eyes glistened. He never thought the word "BEANS" could make him so happy. The lad didn't bother with fetching a proper place to sit. He just sat where he was, can of delicious beans in hand, and excitedly muttered to himself as he began prying the lid off. "Finally-- h-hrk..." A grunt of strain escaped him, just as his hand flicked away from the can. Without a lid within his grasp. Ryn blinked a few times, before wrapping his hand back around the can... "M-mmph... mmmmgh...!!" His eyes were srewed shut, and his eyebrows knit in strain. The sounds that'd squeaked out of his tightly pressed lips were testaments to his efforts-- but alas, they were futile. His hands slipped away yet again, hurting a little this time, and the can was still perfectly intact. He tried opening it again. And again. And again, and again and again... but every time, Ryn failed. And with every slip of his hands, fingers stinging from grating against that blasted metal shell, he felt incrementally more defeated. He was so tired. He tried again. So hungry. Again. Sooo damn hungry... And again. Heat suddenly spread across his face. With utter contempt in his eyes for whatever evil bastard decided to make food so hard to eat, Ryn suddenly shoved the thing into his mouth, and bit down as hard as he could, trying to open it with his teeth. He was on the verge of tears. "Gk... pleeaasshk...!"
