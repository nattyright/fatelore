Buying up and comsuming its resources. Now it was home to a much smaller but still numerous amount of islanders. Even in the slow hours of the day a number of residents still lingered about, scavaging the remains or harking their own wares to passerbys, much to Alex's chargain. Doing so would bring too much attention to them, and despite the noise it was still at a tolerable volume. As long as there was no sudden increase in the population, Alex would continue to live as they did. Occasionally, a pedestrian would catch sight of the strange figure. Wearing a long dark coat over a complex looking mechanical chestpiece and a helmet with a single glowing vertical light down the center. Most knew of Alex as a strange introvert who viciously guarded their territory and steered clear of their path. Those too ignorant or too foolish to stick around too long in Alex's prescence were quickly scattered by a sudden onset of anxiety and panic. To make this difficult journey a bit more tolerable, Alex had stopped at a confectionary to pick up some treats. The cashier was intolerable, nervously laughing and trying to strike a conversation to hide her fear. Alex saw through it all of course, stewing madly in silence until they got what they wanted and left. Eventually, Alex reached their destination, their favorite store on Nirvana.
Walking in, one would be hit by the suddenly silence of the interior compared to the outside. A single middle age bearded man looked up from the register and let out a single grunt. The man's mind was quiet unlike the average islander, was he jaded by experience or perhaps too old to really care? It mattered not to Alex. They approached the front and placed a single mechanical component on the desk, followed by a handful of metal coins and old slips of paper. The man took one look at the items before him, grunted once, and walked into the back of the store. It was the most pleasant interaction Alex had all day. Now they just had to wait for the man to get their part and they could leave this unbearably noisy place and return back to their sanctuary.
