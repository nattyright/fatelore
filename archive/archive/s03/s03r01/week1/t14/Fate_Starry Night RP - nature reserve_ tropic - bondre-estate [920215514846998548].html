Alex Bondre BOT 16-Dec-21 02:34 AM
The Bondre Estate was eerily silent compared to the rest of Nirvana. Even when taking into account the solitary nature of most inhabitants of Nirvana, the Bondre estate was even more isolated and further from the centers of population than most residences. Those who dared to tread near would think the once great estate was abandoned. For the most part it was, save for its lone notorious occupant. Alex Bondre casually strolled through the yard of their estate. To one side, one of their ice golems was carefully tending to the garden. The harvest was looking plentiful this season, food would not be a concern for a long while. To the other side was a flowing river along with a hydroelectric dam which provided enough power for the estate, or at least the parts Alex cared to keep powered. Alex stood by the river and took in the sunlight and ambience as best they could. It was fairly difficult with the metal coffin that was the Helmet of Thought Preservation that was sealed around their head. However, Alex dared not remove it. Even here, in this sanctuary of isolation and tranquility, they could still hear them. The thoughts and feelings of the inhabitants of Nirvana. Muted but ever present. Happiness, Anger, Sadness, Infatuation, Obsession, Terror, Hunger, Pain, Fear, Dread. Alex shuddered as they remembered the collective panic attack the entirety of Nirvana had a few weeks ago. The psychic backlash so great that Alex had to spend the next few days in their specially designed safe room lest their own empathic abilities destroy their mind. Even after weeks had passed, Alex still felt a sense of dread from the event. They had even pulled out an old SERVANT Frame that their family had in the basement. But Alex hadn’t used it just yet. For the moment, Alex was at peace in the blissful quiet ambiance of nature. One which was rudely interrupted by the hydroelectric generator starting to sputter.
Frowning under the helmet, Alex took a look at the machine. The generator was years old with Alex solely maintaining it. One of the components had worn out and needed to be replaced. This was a problem. Not that it was hard to repair, Alex had gotten proficient at maintaining this generator over the years. Not in obtaining a replacement part, they were readily available in the nearby settlement and the run down halls of the estate still contained a generous amount of valuables with which to barter. No the true horror was that Alex had to go outside the estate and travel to the nearby settlement. Walking the roads while bombarded by the cacophony of noise both audio and mental. Truly a daunting task.
