As with most mornings, Koji woke up cold. It was supposed to be early spring now, and for as long as Koji had lived, he'd always enjoyed a temperate climate, with the gentle sun and the warm rains pleasant even in the colder seasons. The temperature had been the first thing he'd noticed when he'd first stepped into Nirvana's #city-docks, the immaculately calibrated comfort of the lakeside's weather dome, the soft sea breeze combing through his hair and brushing against his skin in a touch far kinder than the biting winds of his old post. He wanted to stay here forever, he had thought, if only because the lakeside days were always warm. The nights, though, were always another story. He'd open his eyes as soon as he dragged himself out of his nightmares, turning his head to the side, only to be met with the faint glow of the lamp beside his bed. Shadows dancing beyond his vision. Whispers haunting his mind, mingling with the droning of insects and chirping of night birds outside his window. A cacophony of sounds making his throat constrict with an uncomfortable tightness, lingering regret wrapping itself ever so delicately around his neck. Temperate climates and him do not mix, Koji had come to realize. They were far too forgiving. Far too indulgent. He needed something to keep his subconscious occupied... and what better way to do it than to saddle it with a constant, mild discomfort? Needless to say, it had been almost a decade since he'd dared to spend another night at the lakeside. Bare feet touched icy wood, chasing Koji's sleepiness away with the wintry chill. Cold and lethargic, it took him a good struggle to make his way into the kitchen, then some more to open the electric panel and flip the house's power back on so that he wouldn't freeze to an early death.
Warmth seeped back into the floorboards as Koji went through his morning routine in an orderly fashion. Within minutes, the house felt, no, smelled lived-in again. Toast, coffee and - Koji pulled open the fridge and was greeted with a glorious sight of nothing. Ding! Two pieces of crispy bread popped out of the toaster. With a sigh, Koji hot-fingered the toasts onto a plate, then dragged his body to the other end of the kitchen, where a large mug of instant coffee sat cooling on a table. Breakfast was slightly less enjoyable than usual without the usual sweet spreads on his perfectly toasted bread. He'd emptied out his stock of jam the previous morning in anticipation of his next delivery, but had forgotten to take into account the breakfast on the morning of - the trek to his house wasn't that bad, honestly, but who in their right mind would want to take a dip into the freezing wonderland of Nirvana just to deliver some groceries? But, ah, suppose that wasn't any of his concern. After all, they had a deal, and those lackeys of hers were all too ready to bend over backwards to fulfill their queen's will. Surely their legs could carry them just a bit faster up the snowy trail?
