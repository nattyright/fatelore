He needed that final push, but he struggled to grasp it, for he was being sidetracked by the mind's worst enemy: Confusion. He felt like he was drowning in a tempest of emotions. The tyrant was sure that he had died back there, having been cleaved in half by Octavius' beam of light. However, his body refused to die, as if it were a curse or miracle embedded in his body. Even more odd was the fact that his thought process only returned to him post death. What could this mean? Just who was he? He pried his heads for deeper thoughts. His crown felt heavier than usual as he took it off with much effort. As it rested in his hands, he felt like it belonged to someone else. Memories started to resurface. A battle. A war. He was involved in it. Complicit in it. He could see his swords stained in blood, one representing the sun while the other represented moonlight. He was a prodigy at the art of war, to the point his people praised him fo- No. That felt wrong. Didn't his people scorn him? Mock him? Deride him? He was considered a traitor. William had shown him that. Clearly, that was the truth.
...He repeated to himself, to no avail. He started to regret his newfound memory, envying his previous, bestial state. He shambled towards the tropical hospital, for the red cross engraved on the building gave him a feeling of safety, albeit he couldn't explain why. He couldn't explain anything, in fact. Why his mind felt contradictory to his past. Why he felt like a king yet not at the same time. He desperately searched for any form of connective tissue. Something that would clear the fog in his mind. Eventually, it clicked. It all lead back to her. Both of his memories were united as one as they remembered the woman that inspired them both: Rowena. Rowena was there for him as a queen. Rowena was there for him at war, tending to his horse as she waved him away for battle. He could have not wished for a better wife. Therefore, he could not rest at ease. Not until he found her. If William was his only lead, then so be it.
